      title: Research Directions
      text: |-
        ### 1. Computational Psychiatry
        We develop computational models (reinforcement learning, Bayesian inference) to characterize disrupted decision-making and cognitive control in psychiatric conditions including depression, schizophrenia, and anxiety disorders. We aim to identify transdiagnostic computational biomarkers that can inform treatment.

        ### 2. Cognitive Neuroscience & Neuroimaging
        Using structural MRI (cortical thickness, morphometry) and functional MRI (task-based and resting-state), we study how brain structure and dynamics support cognition — and how these are altered in patient populations.

        ### 3. Naturalistic Behavior Analysis
        Traditional lab-based paradigms capture only a fraction of human behavior. We use multimodal sensors (wearables, video, audio) combined with AI/ML models to study complex behavior in natural settings, enabling ecologically valid measurements relevant to mental health.

        ### 4. Clinical Translation
        We collaborate with clinicians to translate computational and neuroimaging findings into tools that can aid diagnosis, treatment selection, and outcome monitoring in real-world psychiatric care.

        The following projects are open for motivated students to work on. If you are interested, please reach out.

        #### For PhD / Master Students
        - **Computational modeling of depression**: Develop and validate reinforcement learning models using behavioral and neuroimaging data from patients with major depressive disorder
        - **Naturalistic social behavior**: Analyze dyadic social interactions using computer vision and multimodal sensing
        - **Predictive biomarkers**: Machine learning methods for predicting treatment response from baseline MRI and behavioral data

        #### For Undergraduate Students / Research Interns
        - **Data analysis pipelines**: Assist with preprocessing MRI data (FreeSurfer, fMRIPrep) and running statistical analyses
        - **Literature review**: Systematic reviews and meta-analyses in computational psychiatry
        - **Software development**: Web tools for behavioral data collection and visualization
